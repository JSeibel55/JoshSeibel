Stray Cat Risk to Wildlife

This web map was made as the final practicum project for Geography 778, Practicum in GIS Development. To help guide the Dane County Humane Society staff with surrender decisions or recommendations of outdoor, stray and feral cats, they wanted a GIS tool that prioritizes cats for removal from an area (and put into the shelter) if they are at a higher risk of predating on wildlife species, especially if the species is of conservation concern. Ultimately, this interactive web tool allows anyone to enter the location of the cat (either by clicking on the map or entering the address), the tool evaluates the location to nearby sensitive wildlife areas and displays the risk value to wildlife on a scale (low, medium, high value) along with the affected sensitive areas.
